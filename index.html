Automorphic Forms and Arithmetic

  Jan 23 Sameera Vemulapalli (Harvard) Galois groups of abelian varieties and exceptional Tate classes
In 1963, Tate conjectured that the algebraic cycles of a variety can be described in terms of the Galois invariants of certain étale cohomology groups. In this talk, I'll discuss a proof of several new cases of the Tate conjecture for abelian varieties over finite fields, extending previous results of Dupuy--Kedlaya--Zureick-Brown, Lenstra--Zarhin, Tankeev, and Zarhin. This is joint work with Santiago Arango-Piñeros and Sam Frengley.
  Jan 30 Giada Grossi (Paris) From automorphic periods to arithmetic: the case of Hilbert modular forms
The theory of Euler systems, first developed by Thaine and Kolyvagin, has become a central tool for proving cases of the Birch–Swinnerton-Dyer and Bloch–Kato conjectures. Many of the known examples are inspired from automorphic period integrals that capture special values of L-functions. In this talk, I will focus on recent developments for Hilbert modular forms, their p-adic L-functions and some arithmetic consequences.
  Feb 6 Shilin Lai (Michigan) Euler system test vectors and relative Satake isomorphism
The construction of Euler systems often involves delicate choices of local test vectors. Using the relative Satake isomorphism, in particular the unramified Plancherel formula, we give a conceptual proof of their existence in many settings. As an example, we will treat the Gan-Gross-Prasad and Friedberg-Jacquet case uniformly. This is joint work with Li Cai and Yangyu Fan.
  Feb 13 Robin Zhang (MIT) The Stark conjectures for cyclotomic extensions of complex cubic fields
In a series of four papers from 1971 to 1980, Stark proposed conjectures relating algebraic units to the leading coefficients of Artin L-functions. He also suggested an approach to Hilbert's 12th problem and explicit class field theory based on these ideas. This talk will recall the well-established theory for abelian extensions of the field of rational numbers and imaginary quadratic fields before turning towards cyclotomic extensions of complex cubic fields.
  Feb 20 Jacksyn Bakeberg (BU) Excursion functions on $p$-adic $\mathrm{SL}_2$
The Bernstein center of a $p$-adic group is a commutative ring of certain distributions on the group, and it interacts closely with the group’s representation theory. Fargues and Scholze provide an abstract construction of a class of elements of the Bernstein center called excursion operators, which encode a candidate for the (semisimplified) local Langlands correspondence. In this talk, I will present an approach to understanding excursion operators concretely as distributions on the group, with a special emphasis on the case of $G = \mathrm{SL}_2$ where everything can be made quite explicit.
  Feb 27 Kenta Suzuki (Princeton) The constant sheaf on $\mathrm{Bun}_G$
Given a smooth projective curve X and a reductive group G, the geometric Langlands equivalence proved by Gaitsgory, Raskin et al. (roughly) gives an equivalence between sheaves on the stack $\mathrm{Bun}_G(X)$ of G-bundles on X (automorphic side) and quasi-coherent sheaves on the stack of $G^{\vee}$-local systems on X (spectral side). To compute the image of an object under the geometric Langlands equivalence, one usually bootstraps from the Whittaker model. This method fails for the constant sheaf on $\mathrm{Bun}_G(X)$, which is “maximally singular.” Still, we will compute its image under the equivalence, confirming a conjecture of V. Lafforgue. As a consequence, when X is over $\mathbb{F}_q$ we find a spectral description for the constant function on $\mathrm{Bun}_G(X)(\mathbb{F}_q)$.
  Mar 6 Ryan Chen (Princeton) Faltings heights and subleading terms of adjoint L-functions
Colmez's conjecture predicts that Faltings heights of CM abelian varieties appear in subleading terms of certain Artin L-functions. An averaged version is known by the work of Andreatta--Goren--Howard--Madapusi and Yuan--Zhang. We propose a generalized problem relating diagonal cycles on $(n - 1)$-dimensional unitary Shimura varieties and adjoint L-functions of certain automorphic representations of $U(n)$. The case $n = 1$ is (a variant of) the averaged Colmez conjecture. I will explain the origin of our conjecture, discuss our predictions in the general case, and report on our results in the case of $n = 2$. I will outline the comparison of relative trace formulas and highlight some of the new features, such as a notion of "heights of CM $p$-divisible groups". This is joint work in progress with Weixiao Lu and Wei Zhang.
  Mar 13 Yiannis Sakellaridis (JHU) Relative "Beyond Endoscopy" and separation of the Ramanujan spectrum
In the development of the Beyond Endoscopy program to date, a lot of effort has been focused on finding a geometric expression for the "Ramanujan" spectrum of the Arthur–Selberg trace formula, i.e., removing the non-tempered Arthur packets. An early suggestion by Sarnak was to circumvent this problem altogether, by using the Kuznetsov formula instead, where only the Ramanujan spectrum appears. I will report on joint work with Chen Wan, where we directly compare the two trace formulas for GL(n), including explicit calculations of the "transfer operators" that isolate the non-Ramanujan spectrum in low rank. For GL(2), our work generalizes Rudnick’s PhD thesis, and this operator coincides with the Fourier transform on the affine parameter space of orbital integrals, which has already appeared in works of Frenkel–Langlands–Ngô and Altuğ; but in higher rank it has a different form. Further motivation for this work comes from the Relative Langlands program, where there is a hope that non-standard comparisons of (relative) trace formulas could be used to prove conjectural relationships between periods of automorphic forms and special values of L-functions.
  Mar 27 David Helm (Imperial College London) Uniqueness of supercuspidal support for generic representations
Let G be a connected reductive group. Supercuspidal support is an invariant attached to irreducible mod \ell representations of G(k), where k is a p-adic field or finite field of characteristic p. Vigneras showed in the mid-'90s that when G=GL_n, the supercuspidal support of any irreducible representation of G consists of a single conjugacy class, and posed the question of whether this held for other reductive groups; Dudas and Dat gave examples showing that this need not be the case. We prove that for k finite, and G arbitrary, this question has an affirmative answer for a large class of representations called the generic representations of G(k). If time permits we will also discuss how our approach (partially) generalizes to the p-adic setting, and completely generalizes conditionally on certain well-known conjectures in the Langlands correspondence. This is joint work with Jean-Francois Dat, Rob Kurinczuk, and Gil Moss.
  April 3 Stefan Patrikis (OSU) Compatibility of canonical local systems and F-isocrystals on Shimura varieties
I will discuss compatibility of the canonical G-valued l-adic local systems on Shimura varieties of non-abelian type G. In previous work with Klevdal, we settled this for the projections of the local systems to the adjoint group of G, and this talk will focus on lifting the compatibility to G using work of Bakker-Shankar-Tsimerman. I will also discuss the corresponding F-isocrystal compatibility statements, resulting from joint work with Huryn, Kedlaya, and Klevdal.
  April 10 Rachel Ollivier (UBC) On the center of generic affine Hecke algebras
Motivated by the mod p Langlands program, we discuss the generic affine Hecke algebra H and its center. We compute its rigid dualizing complex and identify H as a Frobenius extension over its center with an explicit Nakayama automorphism. An important role is played by a certain commutative subalgebra of H. This subalgebra turns out to be naturally isomorphic to the (quantum) cohomology of a certain toric variety which, in type A, is the Losev-Manin moduli space. This is joint work with Sabin Cautis.
  April 17 Guido Bosco (Princeton) Hyodo-Kato Stacks
Let $p$ be a prime number. In the study of algebraic or analytic varieties over $\mathbb{Q}_p$, a commonly used and powerful cohomology theory is $\ell$-adic étale cohomology for a prime $\ell \neq p$. Less well known and less developed is its $\ell = p$ analogue, called Hyodo-Kato cohomology, which is a refinement of de Rham cohomology. I will present a new geometric approach to this theory, highlight its main features, and, time permitting, discuss some applications to $p$-adic Galois representations in families. A large part of this talk is based on joint work with Anschütz, Le Bras, Rodríguez Camargo, and Scholze.
  April 24 Julia Gordon (UBC) Improving the local integrability exponent for characters of p-adic groups
This talk is based on the joint work with Itay Glazer and Yotam Hendel. It is a well-known result of Harish-Chandra that most invariant distributions on real and p-adic reductive groups (e.g., Fourier transforms of orbital integrals, and characters of representations) are represented by locally integrable functions on the group, and the singularities of these functions are `smoothed' by the zeroes of the Weyl discriminant. We analyze the singularities of the inverse of the Weyl discriminant, and from that, obtain an explicit improvement on the integrability exponent of the Fourier transforms of nilpotent orbital integrals, and consequently, of characters. I will discuss this improvement and some surprising applications. Our methods also explains some instances of rationality of the Fourier transforms of orbital integrals, leaving many open questions.
  May 1 Jayce Getz (Duke) The \rho-Schwartz space and modulation groups
I will explain how to use spectral methods to define the \rho-Schwartz space and prove certain local conjectures of Braverman-Kazhdan and Ngo. With this class of examples in hand, I will then explain how Schwartz spaces for certain spherical varieties are naturally a representation of a group that we christened the modulation group. This provides a broad generalization of the Weil representation of the metaplectic group. We understand these groups best for vector spaces and zero loci of quadratic forms in an even number of variables. In both cases the modulation group is closely related to algebraic groups, and we give a conjectural method of linking modulation groups to ind-algebraic groups in general. Just as in the case of the metaplectic group, the main tool used to construct the link is deformation quantization. This is joint work with Armando Gutierrez-Terradillos, Farid Hosseinijafari, Bryan Hu, Seewoo Lee, Aaron Slipper, Marie Helene Tome, Guodong Xi, HaoYun Yao, and Alan Zhao
